# Content Guide: Evict ICE from 250 Delaware

This guide will help you create compelling campaign content using the Payload CMS.

## Quick Start

1. Start the dev server: `pnpm dev`
2. Go to [http://localhost:3000/admin](http://localhost:3000/admin)
3. Create your admin account
4. Start building pages!

## Recommended Page Structure

### Home Page (slug: "home")

**Purpose:** Immediate impact, clear call-to-action, urgency

**Sections to add:**

1. **Banner Section** (Hero)
   - Bold headline: "Evict ICE from 250 Delaware"
   - Subheadline: "Join us in demanding ICE removal from our community"
   - CTA button: "Take Action Now" → links to `/take-action`
   - Background: Use a bold image or the primary red color
   - Add the campaign logo as a floating image

2. **Full Width Section** (What's Happening)
   - Title: "Why This Matters"
   - Brief explanation (3-4 paragraphs):
     - ICE's presence at 250 Delaware Avenue
     - Impact on immigrant communities
     - Why this location matters
     - What we're demanding
   - Use the accent cyan color for visual interest

3. **Two Column Section** (Quick Facts)
   - **Left:** Key statistics or timeline
   - **Right:** Visual (photo, infographic, or map)
   - Use base-200 background for subtle contrast

4. **Banner Section** (CTA)
   - "Ready to Make a Difference?"
   - Primary action button
   - Use primary red background

### About Page (slug: "about")

**Purpose:** Background information, credibility, context

**Sections to add:**

1. **Banner Section** (Header)
   - Title: "About the Campaign"
   - Brief tagline
   - Use secondary blue background

2. **Full Width Section** (The Issue)
   - Detailed explanation of ICE at 250 Delaware
   - History and context
   - Impact on Buffalo's immigrant community

3. **Two Column Section** (Who We Are)
   - **Left:** Information about organizers/coalition
   - **Right:** Photo of community members or organizing meeting

4. **Three Column Section** (Our Demands)
   - Column 1: Demand #1
   - Column 2: Demand #2
   - Column 3: Demand #3
   - Use cards or icons to make it scannable

### Take Action Page (slug: "take-action")

**Purpose:** Convert visitors into participants

**Sections to add:**

1. **Banner Section** (Hero)
   - Title: "Take Action"
   - Subtitle: "Here's how you can help evict ICE from our community"
   - Use bold yellow background

2. **Three Column Section** (Action Types)
   - **Sign the Petition**
     - Link to petition platform
     - Show signature count if available

   - **Contact Officials**
     - List of relevant elected officials
     - Email templates or talking points

   - **Spread the Word**
     - Social media graphics
     - Share buttons
     - Hashtags to use

3. **Full Width Section** (Attend Events)
   - Upcoming protests, town halls, or actions
   - Consider adding an Events Carousel Section if you have events in the CMS

4. **Two Column Section** (Get Involved Deeper)
   - **Left:** Join the organizing committee
   - **Right:** Donate or provide resources

## Content Tips

### Writing for Advocacy

- **Be Clear:** Direct language about what's happening and what you want
- **Be Urgent:** Convey why action is needed now
- **Be Human:** Share stories and impacts on real people
- **Be Specific:** Concrete demands and actions are more powerful than vague asks

### Using Colors Strategically

Your campaign has powerful colors - use them intentionally:

- **Primary Red (#DC2626):** Urgent CTAs, key demands, critical information
- **Deep Blue (#1E3A8A):** Trust-building sections, factual information, credibility
- **Cyan (#06B6D4):** Clever visual elements, breaking up heavy content
- **Golden Yellow (#EAB308):** Positive actions, success stories, hope

### Adding Media

Upload images to the Media collection, then use them in:

- Background images for sections
- Floating decorative elements (logos, icons)
- Inline within rich text content
- Featured images for blog posts or press

## Section Types Reference

- **Banner Section:** Bold, full-width headlines with CTAs
- **Hero Carousel:** Multiple rotating hero slides
- **Full Width Section:** Main content area, supports floating images
- **Two Column Section:** Side-by-side content (stacks on mobile)
- **Three Column Section:** Cards or features (wraps on mobile)
- **Blog Posts Carousel:** Auto-populated from your blog posts
- **Events Carousel:** Auto-populated from your events
- **Press Carousel:** Auto-populated from press articles

## Next Steps

1. Create your homepage with a strong hero and CTA
2. Build out the About page for context and credibility
3. Make Take Action the most actionable page with clear next steps
4. Consider adding a blog for updates and news
5. Upload your campaign logo and graphics to Media

Need help? The Payload CMS admin interface is intuitive - experiment with different section types to see what works best for your message.
